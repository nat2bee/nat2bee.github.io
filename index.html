I was born in Brazil and since I can remember nature has always wondered me. Then during high school I've discovered the wet lab magic while getting a technical degree in Chemistry (2004-2005). After that there was no way back for me from science and I decided to become a biologist. I expected many things from my graduation studies and my scientific initiation studying environmental biomarkers in fishes (2008-2010) helped me to get even more then I expected. But then came evolution. Learning genetics and Darwin's theory deeply affected me and by the end of my Biology graduation (2010) I wanted to know more about it. In the following year I started my Masters studies investigating mitochondrial DNA from Anastrepha fraterculus cryptic species complex. While searching about the possible evolutionary scenarios I got in touch with bee people. That lead me to start my PhD (2012) in a new lab with a whole new world of bioinformatics and behaviour diversity.
